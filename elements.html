Elements Mall

Interactive kiosk concept.

Project Summary

Client: Savannah College of Art and Design

Role: UX Designer

Skills used: Ethnographic research, stakeholder interviews, personas, visual design , information architecture & prototyping

Introduction

Elements mall was built by the MTR Corporation’s subsidiary Premier Management Service and was established on October 1, 2007. It sits directly above the Kowloon MTR Station and is next to the residential complex Union Square and the Western Harbour Tunnel. Elements mall is built into five zones, each zone is based off the five Chinese elements: Fire, Wood, Water, Metal, and Earth. Along with the many stores, Elements also has restaurants, a cinema, and an ice rink. Throughout Elements there are concierge desks and mall directories to help customers navigate their way around the mall. Elements is a high end mall that “brings together some of the world’s most sought-after brands and services.” We (Nathan N., Thomas T. and Lindsay H.) feel that the directories in the Elements mall in Kowloon can be redesigned to be an interactive kiosk, touch screen functionality, with additional features for better facilitating the customers’ needs to help elevate the overall experience of being in Elements.

Ethnographic Research

To kick off our research efforts my team and I discussed how to handle to ethnographic research by asking questions such as; "What time of day should we go?" and "What should we look for?". After some deliberation the three of us decided that the best approach would be for each of us to individually go to the mall during the morning, afternoon and evening to observe individuals interacting with the kiosks. We took notes as to how many people we saw interact with them during our visits as well as how the people interacted with them.

Personas

After getting a better feel for the layout of the mall and how people interacted with the kiosks our next task was to develop three personas whom exemplified the customers of Elements HK. As a group we went to the mall to interview our stakeholders; the customers, mall employees, residents (there is an apartment complex on top of the mall) and the concierge staff. Personally I felt that this was the toughest part of our process as our primary demographic was Hong Kongers who spoke little English so in an effort to make our stakeholders feel more comfortable we brought a local friend of ours along to translate for us.
