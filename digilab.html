Service redesign:

Savannah College of Art and Design DigiLab.

Project Summary

Client: Savannah College of Art and Design

Role: UX Designer

Skills used: Research, personas, service design methodologies, information architecture, prototyping

In an effort to improve the support system, responsible for the quality of service in 3D printing, our graduate class attempted to address the problem of digital file submission for the SCAD DigiLab.

Process

The problem?

Students seem generally unaware of the DigiLab and it is incredibly difficult for students outside the School of Design at SCAD to use the service. With advice and support from our professor our cohort proposed a single channel solution for online file submission.

Personas

Re-designing the service of SCAD's DigiLab has been an ongoing project with contributions from many students. As such there was already a treasure trove of research; interviews, prototypes and even some rough personas. Diving head first into the previous research I was able to compile enough information to get five workable personas; DigiLab Technician, non-gulfstream users, frequent users, infrequent users & new users.

Wireframes

Above is a concept I proposed as a sort of 'blue sky' opportunity. These sketches detail the public facing website, client log-in and service journey.

Service Blueprint

With newly formed personas in mind my cohorts and I began working out how to improve upon the current solution for digital file submission. Having recently become familiar with Zapier we set up a free trial and began designing the basics of the system.

With the basics of the system in place we began thinking about knowledge access which was one of the biggest pain points our personas faced. The DigiLab's current website is hard to find and doesn't have much in the way of instructional material. These wireframes attempt to address both our primary problem, digital file submission and knowledge access.

The MVP

After checking in with the DigiLab management and getting approval on the progress I began to develop our minimum viable product. In order to accomplish this task I hacked together a solution. Using Wordpress I set up a site with pages for different types of project submissions.

Then I connected that service with Zapier which allowed me to pipe file submissions into Trello as cards the lab techs could process.

Zapier also handled all of the communications so the staff are free to focus on emptying the queue instead of time consuming tasks like emailing updates.
